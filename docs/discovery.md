## 1. EVIDENCE POINTS (Quantified)

### E1: 8 Habits = 24 Years (VA Million Veteran Program, n=719,147)
Practicing 8 healthy habits at age 40 adds up to 24 years for men, 22.6 years for women. Even ONE habit adds 4.5 years (men) / 3.5 years (women). Habits: physical activity, no opioids, no smoking, stress management, good diet, no binge drinking, good sleep, positive social relationships. Low physical activity, opioid use, and smoking had the biggest impact (30-45% higher death risk each).

### E2: Harvard 5 Habits = 12-14 Years (n=123,000+, 34 years follow-up)
Five habits (healthy diet, exercise, healthy weight, moderate alcohol, no smoking) associated with 12-14 extra years and 65%+ reduction in cancer/CVD mortality. Women at 50 with all 5 habits lived 34 more disease-free years vs 24 with none.

### E3: Chronic Disease Impact (CDC/WHO data)
- Diabetes: -11.1 quality-adjusted life years
- Heart disease: -10.3 years
- Stroke: -12.4 years
- Hypertension: -6.3 years
- Each additional chronic condition: -1.8 years average (ranges 0.4 to 2.6)
- CVD accounts for 19M deaths/year globally

### E4: Social/Environmental Factors
- Loneliness/social isolation: equivalent to smoking 15 cigarettes/day, up to -15 years
- Air pollution: -2.1 years average, up to -4 years in heavily polluted countries
- Marriage/close relationships: better predictor of longevity than IQ, social class, or genes
- Education: protective factor (more educated = better health behaviors)
- Stress: 20-30% increased death risk

### E5: Alcohol Quantified (Lancet, n=599,912)
- <100g/week (about 7 drinks): minimal risk
- 100-200g/week: -6 months life expectancy
- 200-350g/week: -1 to 2 years
- 350g+/week: -4 to 5 years
- Regular heavy drinking: -6.9 years
- Combined smoking + heavy drinking: -10.3 years

## 2. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### Competitor 1: Death Clock (The Life Lab)
- **Pricing**: Free trial 3 days, then $9.99/month or $39.99-$100/year
- **Method**: AI trained on 1,200+ studies, 53M participants
- **Features**: 29 questions, biomarker tracking (cholesterol, glucose, BP, heart rate), personalized tips
- **Weakness**: Expensive subscription, mobile-only focus, no bucket list/goals feature, reviews complain about paywall
- **Revenue model**: Pure subscription

### Competitor 2: Final Countdown
- Uses WHO data, lifestyle motivation tool
- Simpler, less personalized
- No monetization sophistication

### Competitor 3: Time Left / newdeathclock.com / deathclockai.net
- Free web calculators, basic factor inputs
- No personalization depth, no goal tracking, no upselling
- Simple novelty tools, low retention

### Competitor 4: Health/Wellness Apps (indirect)
- MyFitnessPal, Noom, Headspace, Calm
- Focus on single domains (diet, exercise, meditation)
- Don't connect actions to life expectancy impact
- Miss the "death awareness" motivation angle

## 3. CANDIDATE JOBS-TO-BE-DONE

### JTBD 1: "Show me my mortality so I actually change my habits"
When I feel invincible but know I'm unhealthy, I want a concrete death date that shocks me into action, so I start making changes before it's too late. The countdown creates urgency that generic health advice doesn't.

### JTBD 2: "Help me build a life worth living, not just a longer one"
When I realize life is finite, I want to set meaningful goals and bucket list items tied to my remaining time, so I prioritize what matters instead of drifting. Time-awareness + goal-setting = intentional living.

### JTBD 3: "Tell me exactly what to buy/do to add years"
When I see my death date and want to improve it, I want specific product/service recommendations with quantified impact ("this adds 2.3 years"), so I can make informed purchasing decisions. Monetization lives here.

## 6. FIVE QUESTIONS FOR SYSTEM ANALYST (Agent 01)

1. **Auth model**: Should users be able to use the calculator anonymously first (viral hook) and then create an account to save results, or require signup from the start?

2. **Calculation transparency**: Should we show users exactly how each factor impacts their date ("+2.3 years from exercise") or keep the algorithm opaque like Death Clock does?

3. **Bucket list social features**: Should bucket lists be shareable/public, or purely private? Social sharing drives virality but raises privacy concerns around death dates.

4. **Affiliate disclosure**: How prominently do we disclose affiliate relationships in product recommendations? Need to balance trust with conversion.

5. **Data sensitivity**: Life expectancy data is health-adjacent. Do we need HIPAA considerations, or does the non-medical-advice disclaimer suffice? What's the privacy posture for storing health questionnaire answers in Supabase?

---

## 7. RISKIEST UNKNOWNS

1. **Legal risk**: Predicting someone's death date could cause psychological harm. Need clear disclaimers. Competitor Death Clock has navigated this.
2. **Accuracy claims**: We cannot claim medical accuracy. Must position as educational/motivational.
3. **Retention**: Death clock is a novelty that could have low retention. Bucket lists and goals are the retention play.
4. **SEO competition**: "death clock" and "life expectancy calculator" are competitive keywords. Need differentiation.
5. **Affiliate compliance**: FTC requires clear affiliate disclosures. Health product affiliates face extra scrutiny.
